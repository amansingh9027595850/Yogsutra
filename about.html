Our Journey

The Story of Yogsutra Yogshala

Bringing pure conscious awareness, structural alignment, and mental tranquility to our community since 2018.

Creating a Sanctuary for the Mind

Yogsutra Yogshala was born out of a desire to build a pure, natural space where anyone—regardless of age, flexibility, or background—could discover the life-changing practices of deep alignment, discipline, and breath control.

Our founder, Sameeksha Pharasi, a Master of Yoga Science and state-level yoga player, noticed that many modern studios focused strictly on the athletic aspects of yoga, stripping away its traditional meditative roots. She envisioned a yogshala that combined precision-alignment instructions with profound mindfulness concepts and yoga science.

Using eco-conscious, sustainable building materials, local woods, and purifying natural plant life, we crafted a studio environment that feels immediately soothing the moment you step through our doors.

Our Philosophy

The Meaning of Yogsutra

Since our clients often wonder about the roots of our name, we look to the sacred Yoga Sutras of Patanjali. A "Sutra" is a thread of wisdom.

What is a "Yogsutra"?

Historically, the word "Sutra" translates to a thread or line that holds things together. In Indian philosophy, it refers to a collection of aphorisms. Patanjali's Yoga Sutras (consisting of 196 aphorisms) serve as the fundamental framework for yoga science.

Our Yogshala (school/home of yoga) was established not just to teach physical postures, but to serve as a living laboratory where these ancient "threads" of yoga science are integrated with modern anatomy, alignment, and mindfulness. Every aspect of our practice represents the practical weaving of these ancient threads of yoga science into daily life.

Nurturing discipline, balance, and bliss in daily life. Walk the path of yoga science with us.
